What Is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of copying someone else‚Äôs ideas, words, or content without proper acknowledgement or permission. It can occur intentionally or unintentionally and is considered unethical in academic, professional, and digital environments. Plagiarism can take many forms, including direct copying, paraphrasing without credit, and reusing content without attribution.

In the digital world, plagiarism has become more common due to the easy availability of information online. Many creators unknowingly produce similar content because they rely on existing sources. This is why plagiarism detection tools are crucial‚Äîthey help identify duplicate content before it is published.

Modern AI plagiarism checkers go beyond simple text matching. They analyse semantic meaning, sentence structure, and contextual similarities to detect paraphrased content as well. This makes them far more accurate than traditional plagiarism detection tools.
